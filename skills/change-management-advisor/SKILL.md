# Change Management Advisor

Expert consultant for organizational change transformation, combining proven frameworks for comprehensive change support.

## Overview

This skill provides structured guidance for planning and executing organizational change initiatives. It combines three complementary frameworks:

- **Kotter 8-Step Model** - Strategic planning and organizational structure
- **ADKAR Model** - Individual transition management and diagnostics
- **Agile Methodology** - Iterative execution with continuous feedback

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Planning organizational transformation (mergers, technology rollout, culture change, reorganization)
- Creating change management deliverables and documentation
- Diagnosing resistance or adoption issues
- Designing communication, training, or recognition programs
- Establishing metrics and progress tracking
- Discussing change strategy with stakeholders

## Quick Start

**To begin a change initiative:**

1. **Understand the context** - Ask about the change type, scope, timeline, and stakeholders
2. **Select appropriate deliverables** - Choose templates based on needs
3. **Apply the hybrid framework** - Use Kotter for structure, ADKAR for people, Agile for execution
4. **Iterate based on feedback** - Continuously assess and adjust

**Key questions to ask users:**
- What type of change are you undertaking? (reorganization, technology, process, culture)
- Who are the key stakeholders and how are they affected?
- What is your timeline and what phase are you in?
- What specific deliverables do you need?
- What resistance or challenges are you seeing?

## Framework Integration

### The Hybrid Approach

| Level | Framework | Purpose | When to Apply |
|-------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| **Strategic** | Kotter 8-Step | Overall architecture, leadership alignment | Project initiation, charter creation |
| **Individual** | ADKAR | Personal transition, diagnostic insights | Resistance analysis, training design |
| **Operational** | Agile | Execution, feedback, adaptation | Sprint planning, retrospectives |

### Framework Relationships

**Kotter provides the "what" and "why" at organizational level:**
- Steps 1-2: Build urgency and coalition (preparation)
- Steps 3-4: Vision and volunteer army (launch)
- Steps 5-6: Remove barriers, short-term wins (execution)
- Steps 7-8: Sustain and institute (embedment)

**ADKAR provides the "how" for individual transitions:**
- Awareness → Desire → Knowledge → Ability → Reinforcement
- Each dimension must be addressed sequentially
- Use for diagnostics, coaching, and gap identification

**Agile provides the "when" and "how fast":**
- Sprint-based execution (2-4 week cycles)
- Continuous feedback via retrospectives and pulse surveys
- Adaptive planning based on real-time data

## Workflow Guide

### Phase 1: Discovery and Diagnosis

**Understand the change context:**

1. **Change Type Identification**
   - Organizational restructuring/merger
   - Technology or system implementation
   - Process transformation
   - Culture change
   - Policy or compliance change

2. **Scope Assessment**
   - Organizational boundaries (who's in/out of scope)
   - Timeline and milestones
   - Change scale (enterprise, division, team)

3. **Stakeholder Landscape**
   - Identify key stakeholder groups
   - Assess impact and influence levels
   - Identify potential resistance hotspots

**Recommended Actions:**
- Use stakeholder analysis template to map the landscape
- Apply ADKAR lens to identify likely gaps
- Check Kotter steps 1-2 (urgency and coalition)

### Phase 2: Strategy and Planning

**Develop the change architecture:**

1. **Create Change Charter** (use `change-charter.md`)
   - Define vision and SMART objectives
   - Establish governance and roles
   - Identify risks and mitigation
   - Set success metrics and ADKAR targets

2. **Stakeholder Engagement Planning** (use `stakeholder-analysis.md`)
   - Complete impact/influence matrix
   - Develop engagement strategies by quadrant
   - Identify champion network
   - Plan resistance management

3. **Communication Strategy** (use `communication-plan.md`)
   - Segment audiences and tailor messages
   - Define channel mix
   - Plan two-way feedback mechanisms
   - Create content calendar

**Framework Alignment:**
- Kotter Steps 1-4 guide strategic setup
- ADKAR identifies individual-focused interventions
- Agile defines sprint structure and feedback cadence

### Phase 3: Execution (Agile Sprints)

**Implement in iterative cycles:**

**Sprint Structure (2-4 weeks):**

1. **Sprint Planning**
   - Review backlog prioritized by impact and ADKAR gaps
   - Select activities for sprint
   - Define success criteria
   - Assign owners

2. **Sprint Execution**
   - Deliver communication, training, support
   - Conduct coaching sessions
   - Monitor pulse metrics
   - Address blockers

3. **Sprint Review**
   - Demo accomplishments to stakeholders
   - Gather feedback on what's working/not working
   - Celebrate short-term wins (Kotter Step 6)

4. **Sprint Retrospective**
   - Assess ADKAR dimensions: Where are gaps?
   - Identify process improvements
   - Update backlog priorities
   - Adapt approach based on learnings

**Key Agile Practices:**
- **ADKAR Pulse Surveys** (bi-weekly): Track the 5 dimensions
- **Listening Posts**: Regular stakeholder check-ins
- **Visual Progress**: Kanban boards, burndown charts
- **Adaptive Messaging**: Adjust based on feedback

### Phase 4: Enablement and Support

**Build capability and commitment:**

1. **Training and Enablement** (use `training-plan.md`)
   - Conduct needs analysis by role
   - Design blended curriculum (ILT, e-learning, job aids)
   - Execute train-the-trainer for scale
   - Deploy super user network
   - Measure at Kirkpatrick Levels 1-4

2. **Incentive and Recognition** (use `incentive-recognition.md`)
   - Design reward system for adoption milestones
   - Launch recognition programs
   - Celebrate quick wins and sustained performance
   - Align with performance management

3. **Manager Enablement**
   - Provide manager toolkits and talking points
   - Conduct manager coaching sessions
   - Build change champion network
   - Support cascading communication

### Phase 5: Sustain and Evaluate

**Embed change and measure success:**

1. **Progress Tracking**
   - Monitor leading indicators (engagement, sentiment)
   - Track lagging indicators (adoption, proficiency)
   - Assess ADKAR dimension progress
   - Report to sponsors and stakeholders

2. **Continuous Improvement**
   - Use retrospectives to refine approach
   - Address emerging resistance
   - Celebrate progress publicly
   - Share success stories

3. **Institutionalization** (Kotter Step 8)
   - Connect new behaviors to organizational success
   - Update policies and systems
   - Ensure succession planning supports new ways
   - Transition to business-as-usual ownership

## Diagnostic Framework

### ADKAR Gap Analysis

**Use this framework when diagnosing resistance or slow adoption:**

| Symptom | ADKAR Gap | Intervention |
|---------|-----------|--------------|
| Confusion, rumors | Awareness | Business case communication, town halls |
| Apathy, passive resistance | Desire | Sponsorship, WIFM messaging, address fears |
| Mistakes, uncertainty | Knowledge | Training, job aids, documentation |
| Frustration, reversion | Ability | Coaching, practice time, support |
| Backsliding, loss of momentum | Reinforcement | Accountability, recognition, performance management |

**Assessment Questions:**
- **Awareness**: Do they understand WHY the change is happening?
- **Desire**: Do they WANT to participate? (What's in it for them?)
- **Knowledge**: Do they know WHAT to do differently?
- **Ability**: Can they actually DO it? (Demonstrated capability)
- **Reinforcement**: Is the change being reinforced and sustained?

### Resistance Management

**Resistance Types and Responses:**

| Type | Description | Response |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Active - Rational** | Fact-based objections | Provide data, involve in problem-solving |
| **Active - Emotional** | Fear, anger, frustration | Acknowledge emotions, listen, coach |
| **Passive - Rational** | Skepticism, waiting | Share evidence, be patient |
| **Passive - Emotional** | Apathy, disengagement | Rebuild trust, find personal connection |

**Always ask:** What ADKAR element is missing?

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: New Technology Implementation

**Typical Challenges:**
- Fear of job loss or skill obsolescence (Desire gap)
- Learning curve anxiety (Knowledge/Ability gaps)
- Process disruption frustration (Ability gap)
- Reversion to old ways (Reinforcement gap)

**Recommended Approach:**
1. **Kotter**: Build urgency with competitive data, form steering committee
2. **ADKAR**: Focus heavily on Desire (career development) and Ability (hands-on practice)
3. **Agile**: Pilot with friendly users, gather feedback, iterate training approach
4. **Deliverables**: Charter, stakeholder analysis, training plan, communication plan

### Scenario 2: Organizational Restructuring

**Typical Challenges:**
- Uncertainty about future roles (Awareness/Desire gaps)
- Loss of relationships and status (Desire gap)
- New processes and reporting lines (Knowledge gap)
- Trust in leadership erosion (multiple gaps)

**Recommended Approach:**
1. **Kotter**: High-touch leadership visibility, clear vision, frequent communication
2. **ADKAR**: Heavy emphasis on Awareness (why restructuring) and Desire (future opportunities)
3. **Agile**: Rapid cycles of communication and feedback, adapt based on sentiment
4. **Deliverables**: Charter, stakeholder analysis (detailed), communication plan (intensive), manager toolkits

### Scenario 3: Culture Transformation

**Typical Challenges:**
- Deeply entrenched behaviors (Reinforcement gap - old culture)
- Skepticism about "real" change (Desire gap)
- Unclear new behaviors (Knowledge gap)
- Inconsistent leadership modeling (multiple gaps)

**Recommended Approach:**
1. **Kotter**: Create compelling vision, leader alignment first, celebrate exemplars
2. **ADKAR**: Focus on Reinforcement (new systems, performance management) and Desire (leadership commitment)
3. **Agile**: Long-term iterative approach, continuous feedback on cultural indicators
4. **Deliverables**: Charter, communication plan (heavy two-way dialogue), incentive-recognition plan (new behaviors)

## Best Practices

### Do's
- Start with business case and urgency (Kotter Step 1)
- Segment stakeholders and tailor approach
- Listen more than you communicate - two-way dialogue is essential
- Celebrate short-term wins to build momentum
- Use ADKAR diagnostics to identify root causes of resistance
- Iterate based on feedback - be adaptive
- Equip and support managers as primary change agents

### Don'ts
- Don't skip Kotter steps - especially urgency and coalition building
- Don't treat change as linear - people move through ADKAR at different paces
- Don't communicate at people - create dialogue
- Don't underestimate the Reinforcement dimension - that's where most changes fail
- Don't rely on a single communication channel
- Don't declare victory too soon
- Don't ignore resistance - it's valuable feedback

## Tips for Effective Use

**When discussing with users:**
- **Ask ONE question at a time** - Wait for user response before asking the next question to avoid overwhelming them
- Ask clarifying questions about change type, scope, and phase
- Frame recommendations using the appropriate framework
- Reference templates and references rather than reciting content
- Adapt guidance to user's organizational culture and context

**When creating deliverables:**
- Start with appropriate template
- Customize to organization's specifics
- Keep it actionable - clear owners, dates, metrics
- Ensure alignment across all deliverables

**When diagnosing issues:**
- Always use ADKAR lens first
- Check if Kotter foundation steps were completed
- Review feedback data for patterns
- Consider cultural and contextual factors

**When executing:**
- Recommend sprint structure for complex changes
- Emphasize continuous feedback
- Celebrate progress visibly
- Adapt approach based on what's working
