# Kotter 8-Step Change Model

## Overview
John Kotter's 8-step process for leading change provides a framework for organizational transformation at the strategic/planning level. This model is used for the planning and structural aspects of change initiatives.

## The 8 Steps

### Step 1: Create a Sense of Urgency
- **Purpose**: Build momentum and motivation for change
- **Key Activities**:
  - Identify potential threats and opportunities
  - Present honest discussions about market/organizational realities
  - Build stakeholder awareness of the need for change
- **Deliverables**: Urgency assessment, opportunity/threat analysis
- **Success Indicators**: 75% of leadership genuinely convinced change is necessary

### Step 2: Build a Guiding Coalition
- **Purpose**: Assemble a powerful cross-functional team to lead the change
- **Key Activities**:
  - Identify key influencers and change champions
  - Build coalition with positional power, expertise, credibility, and leadership
  - Ensure team commitment to working together
- **Deliverables**: Coalition roster, role definitions, commitment charter

### Step 3: Form a Strategic Vision and Initiatives
- **Purpose**: Clarify the direction and required actions
- **Key Activities**:
  - Develop clear vision of future state
  - Define strategy to achieve vision
  - Ensure vision is achievable and aligned with organizational values
- **Deliverables**: Vision statement, strategic initiatives, value proposition

### Step 4: Enlist a Volunteer Army
- **Purpose**: Build broad engagement and participation
- **Key Activities**:
  - Communicate vision widely and frequently
  - Recruit volunteers and change agents
  - Address concerns and resistance proactively
- **Deliverables**: Communication plan, change agent network

### Step 5: Enable Action by Removing Barriers
- **Purpose**: Remove obstacles to change execution
- **Key Activities**:
  - Identify and eliminate structural, process, and skill barriers
  - Align systems and incentives with change
  - Provide necessary resources and training
- **Deliverables**: Barrier assessment, action plan, resource allocation

### Step 6: Generate Short-Term Wins
- **Purpose**: Build momentum and credibility
- **Key Activities**:
  - Identify achievable early wins
  - Recognize and reward contributors
  - Publicize successes broadly
- **Deliverables**: Quick-win roadmap, recognition plan

### Step 7: Sustain Acceleration
- **Purpose**: Maintain momentum after initial successes
- **Key Activities**:
  - Use increased credibility to change systems, structures, policies
  - Continuously communicate progress and vision
  - Promote and develop change agents
- **Deliverables**: Acceleration plan, system change recommendations

### Step 8: Institute Change
- **Purpose**: Embed change into organizational culture
- **Key Activities**:
  - Connect new behaviors to organizational success
  - Ensure leadership development and succession supports change
  - Share success stories and embed in narratives
- **Deliverables**: Culture integration plan, success metrics, succession planning

## Integration with Agile

- **Iterative Application**: Apply steps iteratively, not strictly sequentially
- **Continuous Feedback**: Regular assessment and adjustment at each step
- **Sprint-Based Execution**: Break initiatives into 2-4 week sprints
- **Adaptive Pacing**: Adjust pace based on feedback and progress

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Skipping steps**: Especially urgency-building and coalition formation
2. **Declaring victory too soon**: Before change is truly embedded
3. **Focusing on structure over culture**: Forgetting to change mindsets
4. **Insufficient communication**: Assuming everyone understands and supports
5. **Allowing obstacles to remain**: Not removing barriers that block progress

## When to Use This Reference

Use this model when:
- Designing the overall change strategy
- Planning change phases and milestones
- Structuring leadership involvement and governance
- Identifying potential implementation risks
- Creating business cases and project charters
