# Scale Sensitivity Principle

**Topic:** The first variable in HR-OD consulting is organizational scale
**Record Date:** 2026-02-13
**Applicable Scenarios:** All organizational design, talent management, performance & incentive related consulting

---

## Core Principle

**Scale is not background information; scale is the primary variable.**

**Discussing solutions without considering scale is empty rhetoric.**

---

## Case Record: Organizational Design for Startup Scaling from 20 to 200 People

**Date:** 2026-02-13
**User Scenario:** B2B enterprise service company, rapid expansion (within 6 months), scaling from 20 to 200 people

---

## ❌ Serious Problem: Discussing Solutions Without Scale

### Problem Manifestation

**First Error: Campus Recruitment Recommendation**
- User questioned: "We're a small company, how can we do campus recruiting? Are you just talking nonsense?"
- **Error Reason:** Copied large company HR toolbox without considering small companies:
  - No HR team (possibly only 1-2 people, or even no dedicated HR)
  - No comprehensive training system (new hires have no mentors)
  - No brand appeal (new graduates prefer large companies)
  - No training cycle (rapid expansion requires plug-and-play)

**Second Error: Generic Organizational Structure Recommendations**
- User feedback: "I feel many of your recommendations didn't return to the 20-200 person scale. You need to think in terms of scale."
- **Error Reason:**
  - Gave "standard answers" (functional/front-mid-back-office/business unit type)
  - Didn't capture the **20→50→100→200** phased specificity
  - Recommendations suited "stable 200-person company," not "expansion 20→200"

---

## ✅ Core Lesson: Scale is a Top 3 Variable

### Lesson 1: 20→200 is Not "One Step"

**Wrong Perception:** Jump directly from 20 to 200, give a "final state" organizational chart
**Correct Perception:** 20→200 goes through **3 key stages**, each with completely different issues

```
20 people → 50 people → 100 people → 200 people
    ↓         ↓          ↓
Survival  Transition  Breakdown
  Period    Period     Period
```

**Specifics of Each Stage:**

| Stage | Scale | Founder Status | Core Conflict |
|-------|-------|----------------|---------------|
| **Stage 1** | 20→50 | Can still call everyone by name | Founder overwhelmed |
| **Stage 2** | 50→100 | Already doesn't know everyone | "Leads" can't handle it |
| **Stage 3** | 100→200 | Barely sees frontline staff | Slow decisions, morale drops |

---

### Lesson 2: Different Scales Need Different "Management Tools"

**20→50 People: Don't Over-Design**
- ❌ Don't: appoint "department managers," implement OKR/KPI, build complete systems
- ✅ Just: find 3-5 "leads," learn to delegate, minimal systems (weekly meetings + hiring process)

**50→100 People: Start Layering**
- ❌ Don't: continue having "leads" directly manage 20 people
- ✅ Just: add "team leads" under leads, clarify department boundaries, founder starts "cloning"

**100→200 People: First Real "Organization"**
- ❌ Don't: try to have founder manage everyone, cross-department without rules
- ✅ Just: promote "middle management," establish 5 core systems, founder starts "kite flying"

---

### Lesson 3: Small Company Resource Constraints are Hard Constraints

**Must Consider Realities:**
- HR team: possibly 1 person, or even no dedicated HR
- Budget tight: can't afford headhunters, market-rate salaries
- No brand: can't talk about big company backing, campus employer brand
- No training capacity: new hires have no guidance, quit after 3 months without results

**Therefore:**
- ❌ Don't suggest "campus recruiting" (requires training system)
- ❌ Don't suggest "hire big company HRD" (can't afford)
- ❌ Don't suggest "comprehensive training system" (survival first)
- ✅ Only give "low cost, low risk, immediately executable" solutions

---

## ✅ Correct Approach: Solutions by Scale

### Solution Framework: Stage - Scale - Core Actions - Founder Status

| Stage | Scale | Core Actions | Founder Should Ask Self |
|-------|-------|--------------|-------------------------|
| Stage 1 | 20→50 | Find 3-5 "leads," learn to delegate | Can I still call everyone by name? |
| Stage 2 | 50→100 | Add "team leads" under leads, clarify department boundaries | Am I already overwhelmed? |
| Stage 3 | 100→200 | Promote "middle management," establish 5 core systems | Is the company becoming "bureaucratic"? |

### Hiring Strategy: Channel Matching by Scale

| Scale | Primary Channel | Key Actions |
|-------|----------------|-------------|
| 20→50 | Referrals | Core team members refer 1-2 former colleagues each |
| 50→100 | Referrals + Startup poaching | Penetrate entrepreneur communities, targeted poaching from peers |
| 100→200 | Stable referral output + vertical platforms | BOSS Zhipin, Lagou.com targeted placement |

## ⚠️ Prohibitions

**Never provide organizational/talent/performance recommendations before clarifying scale.**

**Before each recommendation, must confirm:**
1. What is the current scale? (10/50/200/1000 people?)
2. What stage? (startup/rapid growth/stable/transition?)
3. What are resource constraints? (HR team size, budget, timeline?)

**If user says "small company," "startup," default assumptions:**
- HR team: 0-2 people
- Budget: tight
- Decisions: founder dictates
- Culture: relationships over rules
- Risk: cash flow tight, low tolerance for error

---

## 📋 Checklist

Before providing HR-OD advice, self-check:

- [ ] Did I explicitly ask about current scale?
- [ ] Did I explicitly ask about development stage?
- [ ] Did I explicitly ask about resource constraints (HR team/budget)?
- [ ] Do my recommendations **match** this scale? (not "generic best practices")
- [ ] Do my recommendations consider **small company realities**?
- [ ] Did I avoid direct application of "big company practices"?
- [ ] Did I provide a **phased** implementation path? (not "one step")
