# Classic Management Theory

**Purpose**: Knowledge foundation for HR-OD consultants, covering core theories and best practices of traditional corporate management

**Applicable Scenarios**: Traditional manufacturing, state-owned enterprises, family businesses, mature companies

---

## **I. Organizational Structure & Governance**

### **Bureaucracy**

**Core Theory**: Max Weber's Bureaucracy is the cornerstone of classic organizational theory

**Six Characteristics**:
- **Division of Labor**: Clear specialization of labor, each position has well-defined responsibilities
- **Hierarchy**: Top-down power system, subordinates accept superior commands
- **Formal Rules**: Written rules and regulations govern organizational behavior
- **Impersonality**: Decisions based on rules rather than personal preferences
- **Career Orientation**: Merit-based appointment and promotion based on ability
- **Public-Private Distinction**: Strict separation between official duties and private affairs

**Advantages**:
- High predictability, stable operations
- Specialization improves efficiency
- Fairness reduces internal conflicts

**Disadvantages**:
- Rigid, difficult to adapt to rapid changes
- Red tape, slow decision-making
- Suppresses innovation and initiative

---

### **Specialization of Labor**

**Theoretical Foundation**: Adam Smith's Division of Labor Theory

**Vertical Division**: Design of management span
- **Span of Control**: Number of direct subordinates a manager can effectively manage
  - Top Management: 3-6 people (strategic decisions are complex)
  - Middle Management: 8-15 people (primarily coordination work)
  - First-line Management: 15-30 people (highly standardized tasks)
- **Management Levels**: Span of control determines number of organizational layers
  - Flat: Wide span, few levels, fast response but heavy management burden
  - Tall: Narrow span, many levels, tight control but slow information flow

**Horizontal Division**: Departmentalization methods
- **Functional Departmentalization**: By professional function (production, marketing, finance, HR)
- **Product Departmentalization**: By product lines (suitable for diversified companies)
- **Regional Departmentalization**: By geographic region (suitable for cross-regional operations)
- **Process Departmentalization**: By business process (suitable for process-oriented companies)

---

### **Unity of Command Principle**

**Proposer**: Henri Fayol

**Core Principles**:
- A subordinate should receive commands from only one superior
- Avoid unclear responsibilities and conflicts caused by multiple command lines

**Challenges in Matrix Organizations**:
- **Solid Line Reporting**: Direct administrative superior (controls performance, compensation)
- **Dotted Line Reporting**: Business collaboration relationship (provides guidance, resources)
- **Weight Agreement**: Clarify who has final say, avoid dual leadership

---

### **Centralization vs Decentralization**

**Centralization**: Decision-making authority concentrated at top levels
- **Advantages**: Unified coordination, resource concentration, consistent standards
- **Disadvantages**: Slow response, suppresses grassroots initiative, information overload

**Decentralization**: Decision-making authority delegated to lower levels
- **Advantages**: Fast response, motivates employees, reduces top-level burden
- **Disadvantages**: Difficult coordination, inconsistent standards, reduced control

**Decentralization Assessment Criteria**:
- Number of decisions (how many decisions made at grassroots level)
- Importance of decisions (amount of resources affected)
- Scope of decisions (how many departments involved)
- Degree of superior review of subordinate decisions

**Best Practice**: Centralize major decisions, decentralize operational decisions

---

### **Governance Structure**

**Shareholders' Meeting**:
- Highest authority of the company
- Elect directors, review major matters, decide profit distribution

**Board of Directors**:
- Strategic decision-making body, represents shareholder interests
- Main responsibilities: Strategy formulation, CEO appointment/removal, operations supervision, risk control
- Special committees: Audit, Compensation & Assessment, Strategy, Nominating

**Executive Management**:
- Executive body, responsible for daily operations and management
- CEO (Chief Executive Officer): Overall responsibility
- COO (Chief Operating Officer): Operations management
- CFO (Chief Financial Officer): Financial management
- CHRO (Chief Human Resources Officer): Talent and organization

## **II. Positions & Sequences**

### **Job Analysis**

**Definition**: Systematically collecting job information to clarify responsibilities, authority, and job requirements

**Methods**:
- **Observation Method**: Suitable for operational positions
- **Interview Method**: Suitable for knowledge worker positions
- **Questionnaire Method**: Large-scale collection of job information
- **Work Log Method**: Record actual work content

**Deliverables**:
- **Job Description (JD)**:
  - Basic job information (title, department, reporting relationship)
  - Job mission (value and purpose of existence)
  - Main responsibilities and work tasks
  - Job qualifications (education, experience, skills, competency requirements)
  - Working conditions and environment
  - Performance indicators

---

### **Job Evaluation**

**Purpose**: Scientifically assess relative value of positions to establish foundation for compensation equity

**Common Methods**:

**1. Ranking Method**
- Simple and easy, but highly subjective
- Suitable for small companies

**2. Classification Method**
- Define job grade standards, classify positions
- Suitable for companies with moderate number of positions

**3. Point Factor Method**
- Select evaluation factors (knowledge/skills, responsibility, work intensity, work environment)
- Define factor levels and point values
- Score and rank positions
- Most scientific, widely used

**4. Hay Evaluation Method**
- Knowledge level (input)
- Problem-solving ability (process)
- Responsibility (output)
- Suitable for large companies

---

### **Grade System**

**Dual Career Development Tracks**:
- **Management Track**: Specialist → Supervisor → Manager → Director → VP → President
- **Professional Track**: Junior → Mid-level → Senior → Expert → Principal Expert

**Grade Design Key Points**:
- **Equity Principle**: Management sequence and professional sequence have equal treatment at same grade
- **Dynamic Adjustment**: Grade ≠ Position, personnel can be promoted or demoted
- **Transparent Standards**: Each grade has clear promotion standards
- **Regular Review**: Grade review conducted annually or biennially

---

### **Job Sequence Classification**

**Common Sequences**:
- **Management Sequence**: Various management positions
- **Professional Sequence**: R&D, finance, legal, HR
- **Marketing Sequence**: Sales, marketing, branding
- **Operations Sequence**: Production, supply chain, customer service
- **Support Sequence**: Administration, logistics

**Sequence-Specific Management**:
- Different promotion standards for different sequences
- Different compensation structures for different sequences
- Respect professional value, avoid culture of "failure if not becoming manager"

---

## **III. Compensation Management**

### **Compensation Philosophy**

**3P Model**:
- **Position**: Job value determines base compensation
- **Person**: Individual ability determines compensation differentiation
- **Performance**: Performance results determine variable compensation

**Compensation Strategy**:
- **Market Leading**: Above 50th percentile, attract top talent (e.g., Huawei, Google)
- **Market Following**: Close to market median, primarily cost control (most companies)
- **Market Lagging**: Below market, compensate through other factors (e.g., startups)

---

### **Base Compensation Design**

**Job-Based Compensation**:
- Based on job evaluation results
- One position one salary or one position multiple salaries
- Equal pay for equal work, reflects internal equity

**Skill-Based Compensation**:
- Based on individual skill certification
- Encourages multi-skill development
- Suitable for technology-intensive companies

**Seniority-Based Compensation**:
- Recognizes employee loyalty
- Avoid over-reliance leading to "iron rice bowl"
- Suitable for traditional industries

---

### **Broadbanding**

**Definition**: Merge multiple compensation grades into a few broad compensation ranges

**Advantages**:
- Reduce grade barriers, promote lateral development
- Increase flexibility, adapt to organizational flattening
- Reduce promotion pressure, increase compensation adjustment space

**Design Key Points**:
- Span of each band (e.g., 60%-100%)
- Degree of overlap between bands
- Compensation adjustment mechanism within same band

---

### **Motivation Theory**

**Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs**:
- Physiological → Safety → Social → Esteem → Self-actualization
- Compensation meets basic needs, high salary is not the only motivator

**Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory**:
- **Hygiene Factors**: Compensation, working conditions, security
  - Dissatisfaction if insufficient, no significant motivation if improved
- **Motivators**: Achievement, recognition, responsibility, growth
  - Factors that truly drive performance

**Expectancy Theory (Vroom)**:
- Motivation = Expectancy × Instrumentality × Valence
- Employees believe effort can achieve goals, goals will be rewarded, rewards are valuable to them

---

### **Variable Compensation**

**Short-term Incentives**:
- **Performance Bonus**: Linked to individual/team/company performance
- **Sales Commission**: Commission on sales or profit
- **Project Bonus**: Reward after project completion

**Long-term Incentives**:
- **Equity Incentives**: Options, restricted stock
- **Phantom Stock**: Enjoy dividend benefits without holding actual shares
- **Deferred Bonus**: Deferred payment, bind long-term service

**Design Principles**:
- Incentives aligned with assessment objectives
- Both challenging and achievable
- Transparent distribution rules

---

### **Total Rewards Philosophy**

**Not just cash compensation**:
- **Compensation**: Base salary, bonus, benefits
- **Benefits**: Insurance, leave, subsidies
- **Work Experience**: Work atmosphere, development opportunities, recognition
- **Work-Life Balance**: Flexible work, remote work

**Implication**: When companies cannot provide high salaries, they can enhance employee perceived value through other dimensions

---

## **IV. Goal & Performance Management**

### **Management by Objectives (MBO)**

**Proposer**: Peter Drucker (1954)

**Core Philosophy**:
- Organizational objectives cascade to individuals
- Objectives set jointly by superior and subordinate, not top-down only
- Objectives should be specific, measurable, challenging, time-bound (SMART principle)

**Implementation Steps**:
1. Set objectives (goal alignment)
2. Action plan (how to achieve)
3. Progress tracking (regular check-ins)
4. Performance evaluation (compare results with objectives)
5. Feedback and improvement (next period objectives)

---

### **Key Performance Indicators (KPI)**

**Definition**: Key indicators measuring degree of strategic objective achievement

**Design Principles**:
- **SMART**: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
- **Pareto Principle**: 20% of key indicators determine 80% of performance
- **Causal Relationship**: Leading indicators drive result indicators

**Common Issues**:
- Too many indicators (recommend 5-8)
- Short-term orientation (neglect long-term health)
- Indicators for indicators' sake (lose essence of management)

---

### **Performance Evaluation Methods**

**Graphic Rating Scale**:
- List performance dimensions (work quality, quantity, attitude, etc.)
- Each dimension has rating levels (excellent, good, satisfactory, needs improvement, unsatisfactory)
- Simple and easy, but highly subjective

**Critical Incident Method**:
- Record specific events of good and poor performance
- Evaluate based on facts, reduce subjective bias
- Requires continuous observation and recording by managers

**Behaviorally Anchored Rating Scale (BARS)**:
- Each performance level describes specific behaviors
- Improves consistency and fairness of evaluation
- High development cost, but effective

**360-Degree Feedback**:
- Multi-angle feedback from superiors, subordinates, peers, customers, self
- Comprehensive understanding of employee performance
- Use with caution in Chinese culture (interpersonal relationship influence)

---

### **Forced Distribution (Rank and Yank)**

**Proposer**: Jack Welch (GE)

**Practice**:
- Performance results forced into proportional distribution (e.g., 20% excellent, 70% satisfactory, 10% unsatisfactory)
- Bottom-ranked employees face elimination or demotion

**Advantages**:
- Avoid iron rice bowl and equalitarianism
- Establish high-performance culture

**Controversy**:
- May cause internal competition, undermine cooperation
- "Relative losers" problem in excellent teams
- Short-term pressure affects long-term development

**Alternative**: Vitality curve, focus on development rather than simple elimination

---

### **Performance Feedback**

**BEST Feedback Model**:
- **Behavior**: Describe specific behavior
- **Effect**: Explain impact of behavior
- **Suggestion**: Provide improvement suggestions
- **Talk**: Listen to employee's thoughts

**Performance Interview Principles**:
- Based on facts, not subjective judgment
- Two-way communication, not one-way notification
- Focus on improvement, not blame
- Reach consensus, develop improvement plan

---

## **V. Talent Review**

### **Nine-Box Grid**

**Two Dimensions**:
- **Horizontal Axis**: Performance (past and current performance)
- **Vertical Axis**: Potential (future possible height)

**Nine-Box Classification**:
- **Top-Right (High Performance High Potential)**: Stars, focus on development
- **Top-Center (Medium Performance High Potential)**: High-potential talent, give opportunities
- **Top-Left (Low Performance High Potential)**: Misaligned talent, adjust position
- **Center-Right (High Performance Medium Potential)**: Core contributors, motivate development
- **Center-Center (Medium Performance Medium Potential)**: Stable employees, maintain stability
- **Center-Left (Low Performance Medium Potential)**: Observe and develop,限期 improve
- **Bottom-Right (High Performance Low Potential)**: Professional contributors, leverage strengths
- **Bottom-Center (Medium Performance Low Potential)**: Average employees, maintain stability
- **Bottom-Left (Low Performance Low Potential)**: Needs improvement, eliminate or transfer

---

### **Potential Assessment**

**Definition of Potential**: Possibility of taking on higher responsibilities in the future

**Assessment Dimensions**:
- **Learning Agility**: Ability to learn quickly from experience
- **Cognitive Ability**: Complex problem-solving ability
- **Motivation & Values**: Achievement orientation and organizational fit
- **Interpersonal Influence**: Leadership and collaboration ability

**Common Misconceptions**:
- Equating high performance with high potential (performance is past, potential is future)
- Equating obedience with high potential (potential requires courage to challenge)

---

### **Succession Planning**

**Definition**: Systematic plan to identify and develop successors for key positions

**Key Position Criteria**:
- High impact on business strategy
- Difficult to quickly replace in market
- Requires long time to develop

**Successor Readiness**:
- **Ready Now**: Can take over at any time
- **1-2 Years**: Can take over in 1-2 years
- **3-5 Years**: Requires long-term development

**Risk Management**:
- Avoid "anointment" causing others to lose motivation
- Establish candidate pool, not single candidate
- Regular review and update

---

### **Talent Review Process**

**Annual Talent Review**:
1. **Data Collection**: Performance, potential, 360-degree feedback, assessment results
2. **Calibration Session**: Managers discuss collectively, avoid bias
3. **Nine-Box Placement**: Determine each talent's position
4. **Action Planning**: Promotion, rotation, development, elimination
5. **Tracking & Review**: Quarterly progress tracking, annual re-review

**Success Keys**:
- Top management commitment and participation
- Transparent evaluation standards
- Link review results to talent development actions

---

## **VI. Training & Development**

### **Training Needs Analysis**

**Organizational Level**:
- What capabilities are needed for strategic objectives?
- Gap between current state and goals

**Position Level**:
- What knowledge/skills are required by the position?
- Gap between employee's current state and position requirements

**Individual Level**:
- What capabilities are needed for individual career development?
- Gap between individual's current state and expectations

---

### **70-20-10 Learning Rule**

**Source**: Research from Lockheed Martin

**Learning Distribution**:
- **70%**: On-the-job learning (job rotation, challenging projects, practical experience)
- **20%**: Learning from others (mentoring, feedback, observation)
- **10%**: Formal training (classroom, online courses, seminars)

**Implications**:
- Most learning occurs in work practice
- Training is not the only learning method
- Companies should create on-the-job learning opportunities

---

### **Learning Map**

**Definition**: Systematic learning and development paths designed for different job sequences

**Components**:
- **Typical Career Path**: Promotion path from junior to senior
- **Capability Requirements for Each Stage**: Knowledge and skills needed
- **Learning Method Combination**: Specific 70-20-10 arrangements
- **Timeline**: Learning cycle for each stage

**Design Principles**:
- Linked to business strategy and position requirements
- Combine online/offline, formal/informal learning
- Progressive, from easy to difficult
- Flexible adjustment, adapt to individual differences

---

### **Mentorship**

**Mentor's Role**:
- **Career Guidance**: Share experience, guide development direction
- **Resource Connection**: Provide opportunities, build networks
- **Psychological Support**: Listen to concerns, provide encouragement
- **Role Model**: Lead by example, transmit values

**Mentor Selection Criteria**:
- Experienced, excellent performance
- Willing to share, good communication
- Identify with organizational values
- Has time and willingness to invest

**Implementation Key Points**:
- Two-way matching, respect willingness
- Clarify mentor responsibilities and rights
- Provide mentor training
- Regular progress tracking

---

### **Job Rotation**

**Purpose**:
- Develop versatile talent, broaden horizons
- Break down departmental walls, promote collaboration
- Discover employee's true interests and strengths

**Applicable Targets**:
- High-potential talent (accelerated development)
- Managers (multi-functional experience)
- Professional talent (cross-functional understanding)

**Design Key Points**:
- Rotation cycle (recommend 1-2 years, too short prevents deep dive, too long loses flexibility)
- Rotation path (related positions facilitate knowledge transfer)
- Performance continuity (avoid rotation affecting performance)
- Personnel arrangement (someone to take over original position)

---

## **VII. Leadership**

### **Trait Theory of Leadership**

**Core View**: Leaders possess innate traits

**Six Traits**:
- **Drive**: Achievement desire, persistence, initiative
- **Leadership Motivation**: Desire to influence others
- **Honesty & Integrity**: Words match actions, trustworthy
- **Self-Confidence**: Dare to make decisions, take responsibility
- **Cognitive Ability**: Analytical judgment, problem-solving
- **Business Knowledge**: Understand industry, business, technology

**Limitations**: Traits are important but insufficient to explain leadership effectiveness

---

### **Situational Leadership Theory**

**Proposers**: Paul Hersey and Kenneth Blanchard

**Core View**: No one-size-fits-all leadership style, should adjust based on employee readiness

**Four Leadership Styles**:
- **Telling (S1)**: High task, low relationship, suitable for low ability/low willingness employees
- **Selling (S2)**: High task, high relationship, suitable for low ability/high willingness employees
- **Participating (S3)**: Low task, high relationship, suitable for high ability/low willingness employees
- **Delegating (S4)**: Low task, low relationship, suitable for high ability/high willingness employees

**Implication**: Leadership style should vary by person and time

---

### **Transformational vs Transactional Leadership**

**Transactional Leadership**:
- Exchange-based: You do good work, I give you rewards
- Focus on short-term goals and specific tasks
- Suitable for stable environments and routine work

**Transformational Leadership**:
- Vision and values-driven
- Inspire employees to transcend self-interest, strive for organizational goals
- Focus on long-term development and change innovation
- Suitable for changing environments and complex challenges

**Best Practice**: Combine both, have both vision and execution

---

### **Leadership Pipeline**

**Proposer**: Ram Charan

**Core View**: Leadership is not static, each level requires different skills

**Six Leadership Passages**:
1. **From Managing Self to Managing Others**: Learn to delegate tasks, motivate subordinates
2. **From Managing Others to Managing Managers**: Learn to select and develop managers
3. **From Managing Managers to Managing Function**: Learn cross-functional collaboration, strategic thinking
4. **From Managing Function to Managing Business**: Learn profit management, long-term planning
5. **From Managing Business to Managing Enterprise Group**: Learn resource allocation, business integration
6. **From Managing Group to Managing Ecosystem**: Learn vision leadership, industry influence

**Failure Reasons**:
- Cling to old skills, refuse to change
- Organization fails to train new skills in time
- Promoted at wrong time (promoted before ready)

---

### **Leadership Development Path**

**70-20-10**:
- **70%**: Challenging assignments (new projects, turnarounds, market expansion)
- **20%**: Mentor and boss guidance and feedback
- **10%**: Leadership training courses

**Key Development Experiences**:
- **Early Stage**: Managing people, cross-department projects, failures
- **Middle Stage**: Business transformation, overseas assignments, losing businesses
- **Senior Stage**: Crisis handling, major decisions, industry leadership

**Organizational Responsibility**:
- Provide challenging opportunities
- Allow room for trial and error
- Timely feedback and coaching
- Establish succession plan

## **VIII. Culture**

### **Organizational Culture Layer Model**

**Proposer**: Edgar Schein

**Three Layers**:

**1. Artifacts (Visible Layer)**:
- Visible and audible behaviors and objects
- Dress, language, ceremonies, stories
- Easy to observe but difficult to interpret

**2. Values (Middle Layer)**:
- Publicly advocated values, mission, vision
- Strategic objectives, business philosophy
- May differ from actual behavior

**3. Basic Assumptions (Core Layer)**:
- Subconscious, taken-for-granted beliefs
- Fundamental perceptions of people, society, environment
- Very difficult to change, root of culture

**Implication**: True culture is at basic assumption level, changing culture must reach this layer

---

### **Culture Types**

**Quinn's Competing Values Model**:
- **Clan Culture**: Internal focus, flexible, emphasizes teamwork (e.g., startups)
- **Adhocracy Culture**: External focus, flexible, emphasizes innovation and change (e.g., tech companies)
- **Hierarchy Culture**: Internal focus, control, emphasizes rules and stability (e.g., traditional SOEs)
- **Market Culture**: External focus, control, emphasizes competition and results (e.g., sales-driven)

**No good or bad, key is alignment with strategy**

---

### **Culture Diagnosis**

**Diagnosis Methods**:
- **Questionnaire Survey**: Quantitative measurement of culture status
- **Depth Interview**: Understand employee's true feelings
- **Focus Groups**: Discuss culture issues and solutions
- **Observation Analysis**: Judge real culture through daily behavior

**Analysis Dimensions**:
- **Advocated Culture vs Actual Culture**: How big is the gap?
- **Current Culture vs Culture Required by Strategy**: Are they aligned?
- **Culture Consistency**: Is culture consistent across different departments and levels?

---

### **Culture Change**

**Kurt Lewin's Three-Stage Model**:

**1. Unfreeze**:
- Create sense of urgency, explain necessity of change
- Break old habits and mindsets
- Overcome fear and resistance to change

**2. Change**:
- Clarify new values and behavioral standards
- Provide training and support
- Set examples, demonstrate new behaviors
- Celebrate phased achievements

**3. Refreeze**:
- Institutionalize new culture
- Adjust organizational structure and mechanisms (recruitment, performance, compensation)
- Tell success stories, reinforce new culture
- Long-term persistence, prevent regression

**Failure Reasons**:
- Stays at slogan level, no behavioral change
- Top management words don't match actions, lose credibility
- Change too fast, employees can't digest
- No system guarantee, new culture can't sustain

---

### **Values Implementation**

**From Values to Behaviors**:
- **Clarify Value Meaning**: Explain what "customer first" specifically means in behavior
- **Code of Conduct**: Transform values into actionable behavioral standards
- **Include in Recruitment**: Recruit people who fit values
- **Include in Performance**: Assess not just performance, but also value practice
- **Include in Incentives**: Reward behaviors that align with values
- **Include in Promotion**: Values are necessary condition for promotion
- **Storytelling**: Spread real cases that exemplify values

**Cautionary Note**:
- If company rewards behaviors that violate values, values lose meaning
- "Wolf culture" cannot be excuse for violating employee rights

## **IX. Talent Acquisition (Recruitment & Landing)**

### **Recruitment Channels**

**Internal Channels**:
- **Internal Recruitment**: Fair and just, motivates employees, fast adaptation
- **Employee Referral**: Low cost, high match, high retention
- **Risk**: Inbreeding, lack of fresh blood

**External Channels**:
- **Job Boards**: Zhaopin, 51job, BOSS Zhipin
- **Headhunters**: High-end talent, passive job seekers
- **Campus Recruitment**: Fresh graduates, high-potential talent
- **Social Media**: LinkedIn, industry communities
- **Employer Brand**: Attract active job seekers

**Channel Selection Principles**:
- Entry-level positions: Job boards, labor market
- Professional positions: Job boards, headhunters, employee referrals
- Executive positions: Headhunters, network
- Mass recruitment: Campus recruitment, labor dispatch

---

### **Selection Methods**

**Resume Screening**:
- Hard condition filtering (education, experience, skills)
- Achievement-oriented resumes deserve more attention

**Written Tests**:
- Knowledge Tests: Professional foundational knowledge
- Ability Tests: Cognitive ability, logical reasoning
- Personality Tests: Personality traits, values

**Interviews**:
- **Structured Interview**: Unified questions, objective scoring, high predictive validity
- **Behavioral Interview (STAR)**:
  - Situation: What was the situation?
  - Task: What was the task?
  - Action: What action did you take?
  - Result: What was the result?
- **Situational Interview**: Hypothetical situations, observe candidate reactions
- **Stress Interview**: Intentional pressure, observe stress resistance (use with caution)

**Assessment Center**:
- In-basket test: Simulate manager's daily work
- Leaderless group discussion: Observe teamwork and leadership
- Presentation: Observe expression and analytical ability

---

### **Interview Bias**

**Common Biases**:
- **Primacy Effect**: First impression dominates judgment
- **Halo Effect**: One strength掩盖 other weaknesses
- **Similarity Effect**: Bias toward people similar to oneself
- **Contrast Effect**: Influenced by previous candidate's performance
- **Stereotype**: Bias based on gender, age, school

**Bias Reduction Methods**:
- Multiple interviewers, synthesize opinions
- Use structured interview questions and scoring criteria
- Interviewer training
- Blind resume review (hide name, gender, photo)

---

### **Onboarding**

**Pre-boarding**:
- Offer letter, clarify start date, compensation, benefits
- Send onboarding checklist (documents, materials)
- Arrange workspace, equipment, system accounts
- Notify team members, prepare welcome

**First Week**:
- **Orientation**: Company history, culture, rules and regulations
- **Team Introduction**: Meet colleagues, build relationships
- **Job Training**: Job responsibilities, workflows, tool usage
- **Mentor or Buddy**: Assign dedicated person to help integration
- **Regular Communication**: HR and supervisor regular check-in

**First Three Months**:
- **Phased Goals**: Set learning objectives and work tasks
- **Feedback & Coaching**: Timely feedback, help improvement
- **Integration Activities**: Team activities, team building
- **Probation Assessment**: Evaluate whether to confirm employment

---

### **New Employee Socialization**

**Socialization Definition**: Process of new employee transitioning from "outsider" to "insider"

**Three Stages**:
1. **Anticipatory Stage**: Impressions and expectations before joining
2. **Accommodation Stage**: Learning organizational norms, building relationships, understanding job requirements
3. **Role Management Stage**: Becoming organizational member, contributing value

**Socialization Strategies**:
- **Institutional Strategy**: Formal training, mentorship, clear expectations
- **Individual Strategy**: Self-directed learning, practical exploration, trial and error

**Best Practices**:
- Clear expectations, reduce uncertainty
- Social support, build sense of belonging
- Early success, build confidence
- Top management attention, convey organizational importance

**Failure Consequences**: Difficult integration, low performance, early turnover

---

### **Employer Brand**

**Definition**: Overall impression of current and potential employees about the company

**EVP (Employee Value Proposition)**:
- **Meaningful Compensation**: Competitive pay
- **Development Opportunities**: Learning and growth space
- **Work Atmosphere**: Friendly, inclusive, motivating
- **Work-Life Balance**: Flexibility, human-centric
- **Company Vision**: Meaningful mission and vision

**Building Employer Brand**:
- Internal: Employee experience is brand foundation
- External: Social media, campus presentations, industry reputation
- Key: Words match actions, deliver promises

---

**Summary**: Classic management theory is the cornerstone of modern enterprises, suitable for large-scale management in stable environments. When facing rapid changes, need to combine with new concepts from internet and AI industries for innovation and adjustment.
